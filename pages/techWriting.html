Technical Writing

Microsoft Word Manual

Fall 2024

"The Beginner Bookbinder's Guide to Microsoft Word", my first real foray into technical writing. This manual contains around twenty modules. This was a final project for a Professional Writing and Technical Communication class.

Read

Since I was young I have always had a somewhat strange fascination with what I would come to know to be technical writing in its many forms. In middle school I often found myself writing documentation or pamphlets for make-believe inventions that I had, and created websites to house them in Microsoft Word using hyperlinks between documents (which is, surprisingly, very authentic to the origins of websites) and writing copy for them.

When I learned about the Professional Writing and Technical Communication Certificate at UMass Amherst, I quickly realized that it could not only be a great way to diversify my writing portfolio and skills, but could be a way to learn more about these methods of writing that have fascinated me. My time in the program has been extremely rewarding. I have enjoyed learning how to build websites from scratch -such as this very page- as well as the best practices for writing clearly and concisely to communicate whatever idea I need to, big or small.
